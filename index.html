Towards Inclusive, Resilient and Sustainable Agrifood Systems

Armed conflicts across the Middle East, Sudan, Ukraine and beyond have driven some of the most severe food crises in recent memory. This international conference brings together startups, researchers, students and policymakers to explore how climate-adaptive crops and low-tech biotech can rebuild disrupted supply chains in the regions that need them most.

Call for Submissions

Abstract Submission

Every accepted submission receives a citable DOI. The call prioritises Tech-to-Market and AI-Driven Sustainability themes, with a particular welcome for work from the Global South and the startup ecosystem.

Every accepted poster is assigned a unique DOI, giving researchers immediate, citable recognition. All presentations are evaluated by three independent experts, upholding rigorous academic standards. Proceedings carry both ISBN and ISSN, are indexed on Google Scholar and permanently archived on Zenodo. A volunteer editorial team of 120 researchers and practitioners from universities and startups worldwide provides subtitles for non-native speakers and full technical support. Each participant receives a Digital Certificate of Participation with blockchain verification. The conference follows EU Metadata Policies and Standards, ensuring long-term discoverability and alignment with open-science frameworks.

📄 Digital Posters

A0 landscape format. Ideal for ongoing PhD research and startup tech-previews, and the preferred format for participants submitting from conflict-affected regions.

🎬 Video Presentations

10–15 minute MP4 files in English, French or Spanish. Best for early-career academics and innovators showcasing AI in applied settings.

⚡ Rapid Innovation Reports

Concise, structured reports on novel biotech or AI findings, reviewed by the editorial team and archived in the conference proceedings.

🎤 5-Minute Pitch

Fast-paced demo slots for startups and researchers, designed for immediate peer feedback via the asynchronous Q&A forum.

Institutional Partners

Conference Organizers

The Scientiae-Naturalis Conference is convened by an international consortium of universities, research institutions, startups and scientific professional organisations committed to open, evidence-based dialogue on agrifood biotechnology and artificial intelligence. As a Satellite Event of the European Climate Pact, the conference operates under EU metadata and open-science standards, and its scientific outputs contribute directly to the EU statement and call for evidence on biotechnology.

The organising infrastructure rests on a distributed volunteer model. A team of 120 researchers and practitioners from universities and startups across the Global South handles peer review, technical coordination and editorial support — including subtitles for non-native speakers and blockchain-verified Digital Certificates of Participation.

Twelve scholarships are reserved for participants from Low-Income Countries and the Global South, reflecting the conference's founding commitment to inclusive scientific participation, particularly for researchers in regions affected by food insecurity and armed conflict.

Frequently Asked Questions

Have a question?

The conference welcomes submissions from researchers, PhD students, startups, policymakers and practitioners worldwide. There is no geographic restriction. Participants from Low-Income Countries and the Global South are particularly encouraged to apply, and 12 full scholarships covering the APC are available for eligible applicants.
The standard APC is €100 / USD 100. Payment may be made in your local currency or any internationally accepted currency. The exact amount in local currency will appear on the official invoice, calculated at the prevailing exchange rate. Full scholarship waivers are available for eligible participants from Low-Income Countries.
The conference accepts four submission types: Digital Posters (A0 landscape), Video Presentations (10–15 minute MP4 in English, French or Spanish), Rapid Innovation Reports, and 5-Minute Pitch demos. All formats are asynchronous and compatible with submission from conflict-affected and low-connectivity regions.
Yes. Every accepted submission is assigned a unique Digital Object Identifier (DOI), providing immediate, permanent, citable recognition. Proceedings are published with both ISBN and ISSN, indexed on Google Scholar and archived on Zenodo under EU Metadata Standards.
No. The conference is 100% remote and fully asynchronous. There is no requirement to attend any live session. All content — plenaries, tech briefs, posters and pitches — is pre-recorded and available on demand. The Q&A forum operates asynchronously throughout the event period.
To apply for one of the 12 scholarship places, contact the registration team at scientiae-naturalis-registration@yahoo.com before the abstract submission deadline. Include a brief statement of your country of residence and institutional affiliation. Scholarship recipients receive full APC waivers and all standard participant benefits.
Notification of acceptance or revision is scheduled for 15 June 2026, following peer review by three independent experts drawn from the Scientific Committee's reviewer network. All reviewers follow a double-blind review protocol.
All participants receive a Digital Certificate of Participation. The certificate includes your name, submission title, conference details and a blockchain verification hash, providing a tamper-proof, permanently verifiable record of scholarly contribution. Certificates are issued by the volunteer editorial team after the conference close.
