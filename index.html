Towards Inclusive, Resilient and Sustainable Agrifood Systems

The conference serves as a Pay-for-Impact platform. It is the biggest Scientific and Citizens Consultation in Climate Change and Agrifood. We don’t base our professional portfolio on IA, we index the scientific activity. The conference will act a s Data-Driven Lobbying platform, a Scientific Lobbying for EU, where researchers and startups with their data participate in the Report of Consultation and White Paper for the EU DG AGRI and EU DG CLIMA. The conference welcomes startups, researchers, students and policymakers to explore how climate-adaptive crops and low-tech biotech can rebuild disrupted supply chains.

Call for Submissions (See Conference Guidelines)

Abstract See Conference Guidelines

Every accepted submission receives a citable DOI. The call prioritises Tech-to-Market and AI-Driven Sustainability themes, with a particular welcome for work from the Global South.

Every accepted poster is assigned a unique DOI, giving researchers immediate, citable recognition. All presentations are evaluated by three independent experts. Proceedings carry both ISBN and ISSN, are indexed on Google Scholar. A volunteer team of 120 researchers from universities and startups worldwide provides subtitles and technical support. Each participant receives a blockchain-verified Digital Certificate of Participation. The conference follows EU Metadata Policies and Standards throughout.

📄 Digital Posters

A0 landscape format. Ideal for PhD research and startup tech-previews, preferred for participants in conflict-affected regions.

🎬 Video Presentations

10–15 minute MP4 in English, French or Spanish. Best for early-career academics showcasing AI in applied settings.

⚡ Rapid Innovation Reports

Concise, structured reports on novel biotech or AI findings, reviewed and archived in the proceedings.

🎤 5-Minute Pitch

Fast-paced startup and researcher demo slots for immediate peer feedback via the asynchronous Q&A forum.

Conference Organizers
