Agrifood Biotech & AI

Towards Inclusive, Resilient and Sustainable Agrifood Systems

100% Remote Asynchronous Video Interactive Digital Posters Global South Focus DOI Guaranteed Google Scholar Indexed

Armed conflicts across the Middle East, Sudan, Ukraine and beyond have driven some of the most severe food crises in recent memory. This conference brings together startups, researchers, students and policymakers to explore how climate-adaptive crops and low-tech biotech can strengthen supply chains in the regions that need them most.

Call for Submissions

Abstract Submission

Every accepted submission receives a citable DOI and undergoes rigorous peer review, with a particular welcome for work from the Global South and the startup ecosystem.

Every accepted poster is assigned a unique DOI, giving researchers immediate, citable recognition for their work. All presentations are evaluated by three independent experts, upholding rigorous academic standards throughout. Proceedings carry both ISBN and ISSN, are indexed on Google Scholar and permanently archived on Zenodo. A volunteer team of 120 researchers and practitioners from universities and startups worldwide provides subtitles for non-native speakers and technical support for all submissions. Each participant receives a Digital Certificate of Participation with blockchain verification, a lasting and tamper-proof record of scholarly contribution. The conference follows EU Metadata Policies and Standards, ensuring long-term discoverability and full alignment with open-science frameworks.

Submission Categories

Digital Posters

A0 landscape format. Ideal for ongoing PhD research and startup tech-previews, and the preferred format for participants submitting from conflict-affected regions.

Video Presentations

10 to 15-minute MP4 files in English, French or Spanish. Best suited to early-career academics and innovators showcasing AI in applied settings.

Rapid Innovation Reports

Concise, structured reports presenting novel biotech or AI findings, reviewed by the editorial team and archived in the conference proceedings.

5-Minute Pitch

Short, fast-paced demo slots for startups and researchers, designed for immediate peer feedback via the asynchronous Q&A forum.

Governance

Scientific & Organising Committees

The conference is governed by two distinct bodies: a Scientific Committee responsible for academic quality and thematic direction, and an Organising Committee managing logistics, communications and the volunteer editorial infrastructure.

The Scientific Committee upholds the intellectual integrity of the programme. Its members define the thematic scope, oversee the peer-review process and ensure outputs align with EU evidence standards on agricultural biotechnology. All submissions are evaluated by three independent reviewers drawn from the Committee's wider network.

Institutional Partners

Conference Organizers

The Scientiae-Naturalis Conference is convened by an international consortium of universities, research institutions, startups and scientific professional organisations committed to open, evidence-based dialogue on agrifood biotechnology and artificial intelligence. As a Satellite Event of the European Climate Pact, the conference operates under EU metadata and open-science standards, and its scientific outputs contribute directly to the EU statement and call for evidence on biotechnology.

The organising infrastructure rests on a distributed volunteer model. A team of 120 researchers and practitioners from universities and startups across the Global South handles peer review, technical coordination and editorial support. This team adds subtitles to video submissions for non-native speakers and issues each participant's blockchain-verified Digital Certificate of Participation. The post-conference phase includes publication of the Abstract Book and Conference Proceedings with both ISBN and ISSN, alongside the development of a full Video-on-Demand pipeline.

Twelve scholarships are reserved for participants from Low-Income Countries and the Global South, reflecting the conference's founding commitment to inclusive scientific participation — particularly for researchers working in regions affected by food insecurity and armed conflict.
